# Americas OTC/PRN Medication Brand Names & Strengths
*Research for DoseGrid medication tracker — as-needed/short-course self-medication*
*Last updated: 2026-06-20 | Status: 12 major markets covered (US, CA, MX, BR, AR, CO, CL, PE, VE, EC, CR, UY). Remaining smaller markets (PY, DO, GT, etc.) deferred — low marginal alias value.*

> **Note:** OTC status varies by country and changes over time. Items marked `(UNVERIFIED)` need confirmation.
> Users set their own dosing — this file lists brand names and available strengths/forms only.

## United States (US)

### Analgesics / Antipyretics / NSAIDs
- **Acetaminophen (paracetamol)** → Tylenol (325 mg, 500 mg [Extra Strength], 650 mg ER tab; 160 mg/5 mL liquid; 80 mg chewable), Tylenol 8 HR (650 mg ER), Mapap, Panadol (limited US). OTC. [drugs.com/ingredient/acetaminophen.html]
- **Ibuprofen** → Advil (200 mg tab/caplet/Liqui-Gel), Motrin IB (200 mg), Children's Motrin (100 mg/5 mL susp; 50 mg/1.25 mL infant drops). OTC ≤200 mg; 400/600/800 mg Rx. [drugs.com, FDA]
- **Naproxen sodium** → Aleve (220 mg tab/caplet/gelcap). OTC at 220 mg; naproxen 250/375/500 mg Rx (Naprosyn). [singlecare.com, drugs.com]
- **Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid)** → Bayer (325 mg, 500 mg; 81 mg low-dose/baby), Bufferin (buffered), Ecotrin (enteric 81/325 mg), St. Joseph (81 mg). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Diclofenac** → Voltaren Arthritis Pain gel 1% (OTC topical), Voltaren tab / Cataflam / Cambia (Rx oral). [FDA]
- **Combination** → Excedrin (acetaminophen 250 mg + aspirin 250 mg + caffeine 65 mg), Excedrin Migraine (same), Goody's / BC Powder (aspirin + caffeine ± acetaminophen). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Note:** Metamizole (dipyrone) is BANNED in the US — not marketed. [FDA]

### Antihistamines
- **Loratadine** → Claritin (10 mg tab; 5 mg chewable/RediTabs; 5 mg/5 mL syrup), Alavert, store brands. OTC. [goodrx.com, healthline.com]
- **Cetirizine** → Zyrtec (10 mg tab; 5 mg/5 mL & 10 mg/10 mL syrup; 10 mg dissolve tab; 1 mg/mL children's). OTC. [goodrx.com]
- **Fexofenadine** → Allegra (60 mg, 180 mg tab; 30 mg/5 mL susp; 30 mg children's). OTC. [goodrx.com]
- **Desloratadine** → Clarinex (5 mg tab) — Rx in US. [drugs.com]
- **Levocetirizine** → Xyzal (5 mg tab; 2.5 mg/5 mL). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Diphenhydramine** → Benadryl (25 mg, 50 mg tab/caplet; 12.5 mg/5 mL liquid; 12.5 mg chewable). OTC. [benadryl.com]
- **Chlorpheniramine** → Chlor-Trimeton (4 mg, 12 mg ER). OTC. [drugs.com]

### Antacids / GI / PPIs / H2
- **Calcium carbonate antacid** → Tums (500/750/1000 mg), Rolaids (calcium carbonate + magnesium hydroxide), Maalox/Mylanta (aluminum + magnesium hydroxide + simethicone), Alka-Seltzer. OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Omeprazole** → Prilosec OTC (20 mg delayed-release tab). OTC. [FDA]
- **Esomeprazole** → Nexium 24HR (20 mg). OTC. [FDA]
- **Lansoprazole** → Prevacid 24HR (15 mg). OTC. [FDA]
- **Famotidine** → Pepcid AC (10 mg, 20 mg), Pepcid Complete (famotidine + Ca carbonate + Mg hydroxide). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Bismuth subsalicylate** → Pepto-Bismol (262 mg tab/caplet; 262 mg/15 mL & 525 mg/15 mL liquid), Kaopectate. OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Loperamide** → Imodium A-D (2 mg caplet; 1 mg/7.5 mL liquid), Imodium Multi-Symptom (+ simethicone). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Simethicone** → Gas-X (125/180 mg), Mylicon. OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Note:** Ranitidine (Zantac) was withdrawn 2020 (NDMA); Zantac 360 is now famotidine. Hyoscine/scopolamine oral (Buscopan) NOT marketed in US (only transdermal Transderm Scop for motion sickness, Rx). [FDA]

### Decongestants / Cough / Cold
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Sudafed (30 mg, 120 mg ER, 240 mg ER). Behind-the-counter (BTC), photo-ID required (Combat Methamphetamine Act). [FDA]
- **Phenylephrine** → Sudafed PE (10 mg) — note FDA 2023 advisory: oral PE found ineffective as decongestant. OTC. [FDA]
- **Dextromethorphan** → Delsym (30 mg/5 mL ER susp), Robitussin DM (DXM + guaifenesin), Mucinex DM. OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Guaifenesin** → Mucinex (600 mg, 1200 mg ER tab), Robitussin (100 mg/5 mL). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Combination cold** → DayQuil/NyQuil (acetaminophen + DXM ± doxylamine ± phenylephrine), Theraflu. OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Note:** Ambroxol & bromhexine NOT marketed in the US.

### Sleep / Antiemetic / Motion

- **Diphenhydramine (sleep)** → ZzzQuil (25 mg), Unisom SleepGels (50 mg), Tylenol PM / Advil PM (analgesic + diphenhydramine). OTC. [drugs.com]

- **Dimenhydrinate** → Dramamine Original (50 mg). OTC. [drugs.com]
- **Meclizine** → Dramamine Less Drowsy (25 mg), Bonine (25 mg). OTC. [drugs.com]

## Canada (CA)

### Analgesics / Antipyretics / NSAIDs
- **Acetaminophen** → Tylenol Regular (325 mg), Tylenol Extra Strength (500 mg), Tylenol 8HR Arthritis (650 mg ER), Children's Tylenol (160 mg/5 mL), Tempra, Atasol. OTC. [Choosing Wisely Canada, chatelaine.com]
- **Acetaminophen + codeine** → Tylenol No. 1 (acetaminophen 300 mg + codeine 8 mg + caffeine 15 mg) — historically "behind-the-counter" exempted-codeine product; many provinces now require Rx (low-dose codeine OTC sales restricted/eliminated in most provinces). [Health Canada]
- **Ibuprofen** → Advil (200 mg tab/caplet/Liqui-Gel), Motrin IB (200 mg), Children's Advil/Motrin (100 mg/5 mL). OTC ≤400 mg; higher Rx. [Arthritis Research Canada]
- **Naproxen sodium** → Aleve (220 mg). OTC at 220 mg; naproxen 250/375/500 mg Rx (Naprosyn). [arthritisresearch.ca]
- **ASA (aspirin)** → Aspirin/Bayer (325 mg, 500 mg; 81 mg low-dose), Entrophen (enteric-coated). OTC. [medxdrg.com]
- **Diclofenac** → Voltaren Emulgel 1.16%/2.32% (topical, OTC). Oral diclofenac (Voltaren) Rx. [medxdrg.com]
- **Combination** → Excedrin (acetaminophen + ASA + caffeine), 222 (ASA 375 mg + caffeine 30 mg + codeine 8 mg — exempted/restricted). OTC where allowed. [Health Canada]
- **Note:** Metamizole (dipyrone) is NOT marketed in Canada. [Health Canada]

### Antihistamines
- **Loratadine** → Claritin (10 mg tab; 5 mg/5 mL syrup; 10 mg RapidDissolve). OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Cetirizine** → Reactine (5 mg, 10 mg tab; liquid gels) [Canadian brand name for cetirizine, = US Zyrtec]. OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Fexofenadine** → Allegra (60 mg, 120 mg, 180 mg). OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Desloratadine** → Aerius (5 mg tab; 0.5 mg/mL syrup). OTC in Canada. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Diphenhydramine** → Benadryl (25 mg, 50 mg; 12.5 mg/5 mL liquid; topical). OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Chlorpheniramine** → Chlor-Tripolon (4 mg, 12 mg ER). OTC. [Health Canada DPD]

### Antacids / GI / PPIs / H2
- **Antacids** → Tums (calcium carbonate 500–1000 mg), Rolaids, Maalox, Gaviscon (alginate + antacid), Diovol, Pepto-Bismol (bismuth subsalicylate). OTC. [Health Canada]
- **Omeprazole** → Olex (omeprazole 20 mg) — OTC PPI in Canada. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Esomeprazole** → Nexium 24HR (20 mg). OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Pantoprazole** → Pantoloc / Tecta — Rx in Canada (not OTC). [Health Canada DPD]
- **Famotidine** → Pepcid AC (10 mg, 20 mg), Pepcid Complete. OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Loperamide** → Imodium (2 mg caplet; liquid). OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Simethicone** → Ovol (40/80/125 mg), Gas-X. OTC. [Health Canada DPD]
- **Note:** Hyoscine butylbromide (Buscopan 10 mg tab) is available OTC in Canada. [Health Canada DPD]

### Decongestants / Cough / Cold
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Sudafed (60 mg; 120/240 mg ER). OTC but kept behind-the-counter in many provinces (precursor controls). [medxdrg.com]
- **Phenylephrine** → Sudafed PE (10 mg). OTC. [Health Canada]
- **Dextromethorphan** → Benylin DM, Robitussin DM, Buckley's (also herbal/menthol formulas), Balminil. OTC. [en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buckley's]
- **Guaifenesin** → Robitussin, Benylin (expectorant), Mucinex. OTC. [Health Canada]
- **Combination** → NeoCitran (hot drink: acetaminophen + pheniramine + phenylephrine), Tylenol Cold, Advil Cold & Sinus (ibuprofen + pseudoephedrine, behind counter). OTC. [Health Canada]
- **Note:** Ambroxol is NOT a common Canadian brand; bromhexine not marketed.

### Sleep / Antiemetic / Motion
- **Melatonin** → Natural Factors, Jamieson, Webber Naturals (1/3/5/10 mg) — Natural Health Product. OTC. [Health Canada NHPD]
- **Dimenhydrinate** → Gravol (50 mg tab; 15 mg/5 mL & 3 mg/mL liquid; chewable; kids; suppositories 25/50/100 mg) — iconic Canadian brand. OTC (may be behind counter). [Gravol, Health Canada]
- **Diphenhydramine (sleep)** → Nytol (25/50 mg), Sleep-Eze, Unisom (diphenhydramine in Canada), Tylenol Nighttime. OTC. [Health Canada]
- **Doxylamine** → Unisom-2 (doxylamine in some formats); Diclectin (doxylamine + pyridoxine) is Rx for pregnancy nausea. [Health Canada]
- **Dimenhydrinate** also used as antiemetic (Gravol, above). [Health Canada]
