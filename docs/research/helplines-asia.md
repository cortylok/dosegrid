> **Confidence scale:**  
> - `high` = confirmed from an official government / hospital / WHO source within ~2 years

## Country notes

### East Asia

- **Japan:** Both Osaka (+81-72-727-2499) and Tsukuba (+81-29-852-9999) lines answer in Japanese only. Non-Japanese speakers should dial 119 and request a medical interpreter, or contact the nearest hospital emergency.
- **China:** No single national public poison hotline confirmed. The 120 system (general emergency medical) is the primary route; hospital A&E departments handle poisoning. NIOHP (+86-10-83132399) is a professional/institutional contact.
- **South Korea:** 1339 is the key multilingual public medical hotline (English, Chinese, Japanese). The Seoul Poison Control Center (1855-2221) is a specialist resource but weekday-hours only.
- **Taiwan:** Poison center is at Taipei Veterans General Hospital (VGHTPE). The CDC communicable disease hotline 1922 is a separate public health line.
- **Hong Kong:** HKPIC is healthcare-professionals-only. General public should call 999. The Hong Kong Poison Control Centre (pcc.org.hk) opened 2023 for clinical care but does not publish a separate public advice number.

### Southeast Asia

- **Indonesia:** BPOM's SiKer (Poison Information System) role was transferred after 2018 regulation change; BPOM's Halo BPOM (1500-533) handles drug/food consumer queries including poison information during business hours. For medical emergencies, call 112 or 119 (ext 9 for general health queries).
- **Malaysia:** PRN hotline (04-6536-999) has limited hours. Outside those hours, call 999.
- **Brunei:** No dedicated national poison centre. Direct RIPAS Hospital A&E at +673-2-242-424.

### South Asia

- **India:** Multiple toll-free centres. The AIIMS Delhi line (1800-116-117) is the national anchor, operating 24/7. State 104 lines (health advice) exist in most states including Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Rajasthan, etc.
- **Pakistan:** No public poison hotline confirmed. In emergencies, call 115 (ambulance) or go to nearest hospital. Aga Khan University Hospital is the closest to a national reference centre.
- **Bangladesh:** No confirmed dedicated public poison centre or hotline. Call 999 for emergencies.
- **Nepal:** The TUTH Poison Information Centre (01-4502011) was established December 2023 — relatively new; verify availability before publishing.

### Central Asia

- **Kazakhstan:** KazToxBase opened June 2024 at the Medical Center of the President's Administration. No confirmed public phone number found; verify via Ministry of Health (egov.kz).
- **Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan:** No confirmed national poison centres. Use 112 (general emergency).

### Middle East / Gulf

- **Turkey:** ALO 114 (UZEM) is a well-established national public line, ~220,000 calls/year.
- **Israel:** IPIC phone is 04-777-1900 (confirmed via Rambam official site — different from older sources citing 04-777-4444).
- **Lebanon:** USJ Centre for Poison Control (+961-1-421-259) is a 24 h public resource. Lebanon's MoPH runs the general health hotline 1214.
- **Jordan:** Pharmacy One™ (not a government body) operates the de-facto public poison call centre (+962-79-171-2222).
- **Kuwait:** KPCC (1804774) opened to the public Feb 2024; previously clinician-only.
- **Qatar:** Qatar Poison Center (4003-1111) and PHCC's 16000 line are both high-quality, well-documented public services.
- **Saudi Arabia:** 937 (MOH) is the best public option for medical advice including overdose/poisoning guidance. King Fahad Medical City poison dept. is being expanded to the Riyadh public.
- **UAE (Abu Dhabi):** 800-424 is confirmed and public. Dubai: no separate poison line found; use 998 (ambulance) or 800-DHA (800-342) for health queries.
- **Iran:** Loghman-Hakim in Tehran is the world's busiest poison centre; reached via the emergency system (115) or directly. Public can call the hospital.
- **Iraq, Syria, Yemen:** Active conflict or infrastructure collapse means no confirmed functional national poison lines. Rely on local emergency numbers and hospital A&E.
