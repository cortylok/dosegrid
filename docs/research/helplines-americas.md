# Emergency, Poison & Health Helplines — Americas

> **Last updated:** 2026-06-18  
> **Research methodology:** Official government/health ministry websites, PAHO/WHO directory, RETOXLAC 2024 directory, REDCIATOX network, national poison-centre websites, America's Poison Centers (AAPCC), Health Canada, country-level emergency-service pages.  
> **CRITICAL WARNING:** These numbers are provided for a medication-safety app. In any suspected poisoning or overdose, call the local emergency number FIRST. Numbers can change — verify with local authorities. Do NOT rely solely on this table in a live emergency.

---

## How to read this table

| Column | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Emergency** | General emergency / ambulance number |
| **Poison centre** | Name of the national/main toxicological information centre |
| **Poison number** | Public telephone number for the poison centre |
| **Public-callable?** | `yes` = open to general public (24 h unless noted); `no` = health professionals only |
| **Nurse/health line** | Non-emergency public health advice line |
| **Confidence** | `high` = confirmed from official/government source; `med` = confirmed from authoritative secondary source (RETOXLAC, PAHO, regional health authority); `low` = unconfirmed, single anecdotal source, or no dedicated service known |

## Notes and Country Details

### North America

**USA (US):** 1-800-222-1222 is the single national Poison Help line, operated by America's Poison Centers (AAPCC). Answered 24 h by toxicologists. 911 for all emergencies.

**Canada (CA):** 1-844-764-7669 was launched March 2023 and routes to the caller's regional poison centre anywhere in Canada. Québec still uses its own provincial line 1-800-463-5060. 811 is the Health Link nurse line available in most provinces/territories (not all — check individual province). Both 1-844 and 811 are free, 24 h.

**Mexico (MX):** No single national free poison line. RETOMEX coordinates 21+ regional centres. Mexico City IMSS centre: 55-5238-2781. In an emergency call 911. Cruz Roja (Red Cross) ambulance 065.

---

### Central America

**Guatemala (GT):** CIAT-USAC provides 24 h public poison-information service. Toll-free line 1-801-0029832 confirmed as active. Guatemala does not have 911 as a unified number — use 110 (police), 122/123 (fire/rescue), 125 (Cruz Roja ambulance).

**Belize (BZ):** No dedicated poison centre identified. Karl Heusner Memorial Hospital (+501 223-1548) is the main referral centre. 911 is the general emergency number.

**El Salvador (SV):** CIATOX (REDCIATOX member) at Hospital Zacamil. 911 is the national emergency number (launched 2016). CIATOX +503 6027-1459 is the 24 h advisory number — note this centre experienced service gaps in prior years; if uncertain, call 911 and request toxicology.

**Honduras (HN):** CENTOX (UNAH) provides 24 h public service on (504) 2216-5166/7 and WhatsApp (504) 3392-4403. 911 is the national emergency number.

**Nicaragua (NI):** CIVATOX (MINSA) +505 2894-7990 is confirmed via RETOXLAC. Emergency ambulance is Cruz Roja 128; police 118; fire 115.

**Costa Rica (CR):** CNCI is the strongest poison centre in Central America. Toll-free 800-4686-9422 (800-INTOXICA) is fully publicly accessible 24 h/365. 911 unified emergency.

**Panama (PA):** SUME 911 is Panama's unified emergency system since 2007. No confirmed dedicated public poison line — Hospital Santo Tomás toxicology unit is the national referral. Call 911.

---

### Caribbean

**Cuba (CU):** CENATOX (http://www.cenatox.sld.cu/) is the national toxicology centre. Havana numbers confirmed via multiple Cuban health sources. Ambulance: 104.

**Jamaica (JM):** CARPIN (Caribbean Poison Information Network) has been reorganising; 1-888-POISON and the UWI Mona extension are the most commonly cited public lines. Ambulance/fire: 110.

**Haiti (HT):** No functioning dedicated poison centre confirmed. Emergency services severely impacted by gang activity; the National Ambulance Centre (CAN) has experienced closures. Current stable emergency number: 114. Situation volatile — verify with current travel advisories.

**Dominican Republic (DO):** INTOXDOM advisory centre was formally launched at Hospital General Plaza de la Salud but a confirmed direct public line has not been published. Use 911 (national, multilingual). 911 system launched 2014–2017.

**Trinidad and Tobago (TT):** UWI PIC at St. Augustine is the main regional poison resource. Phone (868) 662-2002 is the UWI main switchboard — ask for the Poison Information Centre. Dedicated direct number not confirmed. Ambulance: 811.

**Caribbean small states (AG, DM, GD, KN, LC, VC):** None of these have confirmed standalone poison centres. In a poisoning emergency, call the local general emergency number (most use 911 or 999) and request immediate hospital transfer. The UWI PIC in Trinidad can provide telephone advice: (868) 662-2002.

### South America

**Brazil (BR):** 0800-722-6001 routes to the nearest CIATox member centre (ANVISA-regulated national network). SAMU 192 for ambulance. Disque Saúde 136 is a general health information/complaints line (not a nurse triage line, but publicly accessible and free, 24 h automated / staffed weekdays).

**Argentina (AR):** Three major free poison lines: (1) CNI national — 0800-333-0160; (2) Italian Hospital CETOX — 0800-444-4400; (3) Buenos Aires Province — 0800-444-8694. All are 24 h. 107 is SAME ambulance in Buenos Aires city; 911 is the national emergency number across all provinces.

**Chile (CL):** CITUC is the only recognised national poison centre in Chile. Numbers confirmed from the official CITUC website. 131 = SAMU ambulance; 132 = fire; 133 = Carabineros (police).

**Uruguay (UY):** CIAT 1722 is the confirmed public 24 h line. 105 = SAMU ambulance; 911 = all emergencies.

**Paraguay (PY):** CNTox at the national emergency medical centre. Number (595-21) 220-418 confirmed via RETOXLAC and PAHO directories.

**Bolivia (BO):** No confirmed public national poison line identified in current sources. Emergency: 118 (ambulance), 110 (police), 119 (fire). Recommend calling 118 and requesting hospital toxicology service.

**Peru (PE):** CICOTOX (UNMSM) is Peru's only 24 h public poison centre. Free line 0800-1-3040 confirmed. SAMU 106 for ambulance.

**Ecuador (EC):** CIATOX national free line 1800-VENENO (1800-836-366) is confirmed from the Ministry of Health official site. 911 for emergencies.

**Colombia (CO):** CISPROQUIM 018000-916-012 (toll-free, 24 h) is the main chemical/poison information line. 123 = NUSE (unified police/ambulance/fire). SAMU ambulance: 132. Línea 106 = Ministry of Health mental-health crisis line (not nurse triage, but publicly accessible 24 h).

**Venezuela (VE):** SIMET/CIATO at UCV: 0800-TOXICOS (0800-8694267). Chemical emergencies (industrial/pesticide): CISPROQUIM 0800-1005-012. Situation: infrastructure quality variable due to economic conditions — 911/171 may have coverage gaps outside Caracas.

**Guyana (GY):** 913 is the ambulance number but is geographically limited to Georgetown and surroundings. GPHC is the national hospital. No poison centre.

**Suriname (SR):** 113 = ambulance (SUR-CAD). No dedicated poison centre.

**French Guiana (GF):** Overseas department of France. Uses French emergency system (15/18/17/112). Poison control line for the French Caribbean/Guiana region: 0596-75-15-75 (Guadeloupe CHU CAPTV).
