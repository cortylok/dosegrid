## Important Operational Notes

1. **South Africa 0861 555 777** — This is the continent's best-resourced public poison helpline. It is operated 24 h/7 d by the AfriTox network (Tygerberg Poison Information Centre + Red Cross War Memorial Children's Hospital), is free to call from within South Africa, and is confirmed active as of 2025. Also reachable at +27 21 658 5308 (office hours only). **Ambulance: 10177; Mobile emergency: 112.**

2. **Algeria** — Centre Antipoisons d'Alger, CHU Bab el Oued, Boulevard Said Touati. +213 21 97 98 98. 24 h. Algeria hosts the headquarters of the African Network of Poison Control Centres (ANPCC), launched in 2018.

3. **Morocco** — CAPM in Rabat is one of Africa's most capable centres, operating 24 h with clinical toxicologists. Primary contact from official site: +212 537 77 71 69. Toll-free national line (from Morocco): 0801 00 18 01. Ambulance/SAMU: 15. Also call 112 from a mobile.

4. **Senegal** — Centre Antipoison du Sénégal, CHU de Fann, Dakar. Emergency: +221 818 00 15 15. Office: +221 33 825 40 07. The national SAMU (1515) also handles acute medical emergencies.

5. **Kenya** — Two 24 h centres both public-callable: (a) KNH toll-free 0800 720 021; (b) Gertrude's Children's Hospital +254 708 988 400. General emergency: 999 / 112 / 911.

6. **Egypt** — Three university-based centres: Ain Shams (022 434 6127), Cairo University (022 364 0402), Alexandria (034 862 244). General emergency: 123 for ambulance; 112 all services.

7. **Ghana** — Poisons Information and Control Centre at Ridge Hospital (Greater Accra Regional Hospital), Castle Road, Accra. Emergency toll-free: +233 800 100 46. Also +233 302 244 733. 24 h.

8. **Zimbabwe** — DaTIS at University of Zimbabwe / Parirenyatwa Hospital, Harare. Number from WHO 2019 directory: +263 4 307 148. Primarily serves health professionals but also public. Current status should be verified.

9. **Ethiopia** — Saint Peter's Specialized Hospital Toxicology Center, Addis Ababa (opened April 2017). Contacts: +251 911 480 507 / +251 900 571 757. Go to major hospital in absence of response.

10. **Nigeria** — No legacy national poison centre. A new Toxicology Information Centre opened at AUST, Abuja (December 2023) under RMRDC, but a publicly-callable emergency number has not been published. NAFDAC consumer safety line: 0800 162 3322 (pharmacovigilance reporting, not acute emergency). Emergency: 112.

11. **Tanzania** — The number 0800 117 188 appeared in one secondary travel source; it could not be confirmed from official Tanzanian government sources and is listed at low confidence.

12. **Libya, Sudan, South Sudan, Somalia, CAR, DRC** — These countries face severe infrastructure or conflict constraints. Emergency numbers may be unreliable. Users should seek the nearest functioning hospital.

13. **Most sub-Saharan African countries** — The safest advice for medication overdose is: call the country's ambulance/SAMU number, then proceed to the nearest hospital emergency department. Fewer than half of WHO Africa Member States had a functioning poison centre as of 2019 (WHO AFRO).
