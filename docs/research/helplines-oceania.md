## Notes

1. **No Pacific island nation outside Australia, New Zealand, and US territories has an operational national poison centre.** For all other countries listed above, the recommended action for poisoning is to call the national emergency number and proceed to the nearest hospital. Guidance from regional health bodies (WHO WPRO, SPC/PPHSN) suggests calling Australia's centre (13 11 26) or NZ's centre (0800 764 766) as the nearest English-language expert resource — but neither centre has a formal mandate for calls from non-residents and international callback is not guaranteed.

2. **FSM** is an explicit exception: the Nebraska Regional Poison Center has a formal arrangement and a published landline number (402-955-5555) for FSM callers.

3. **Guam and American Samoa** are covered by the US 1-800-222-1222 system (Oregon Poison Center for Guam; Nebraska Regional Poison Center for American Samoa).

4. **French territories** (New Caledonia, French Polynesia, Wallis & Futuna) should access poison advice via SAMU 15, which can consult with French mainland centres.

5. **Papua New Guinea** uses St John Ambulance (111) as the primary nationwide emergency medical service; Port Moresby General Hospital ER is +675 324 8200.

6. **Samoa** changed its emergency number system in 2018; 911 replaced legacy numbers 994, 995, 996. Legacy numbers may still redirect but 911 is the official number.

7. **Kiribati** and **Tuvalu** emergency number data comes from multiple slightly conflicting sources; confidence is medium. The most consistently cited primary number for each is 999.

8. **Tokelau** has no airstrip; medical evacuation requires a multi-day boat journey to Samoa or NZ — there is no formal emergency dial number comparable to other nations.
