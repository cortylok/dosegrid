## Notes on specific entries

### Emergency number
- **112** is the pan-European emergency number and is confirmed active in every country in this table. Some countries also retain their own legacy numbers (999 in UK/IE, 103 in RU/BY/AM/AZ, 103 in GE, 127 in AL, 194 in HR/RS/XK).

### UK
- NPIS does **not** take public calls. Public should call **111** (England, Wales) or **999** for life-threatening situations.

### Ireland
- Public NPIC line operates **8am–10pm daily**. Outside those hours, call 112/999.

### Malta
- MNPC public line is **08:00–20:00 daily**. HCPs can reach the centre 24/7. Outside hours, call 112.

### Netherlands
- NVIC is **clinicians only**. The general public must call 112 for poisoning emergencies.

### France
- Any of the 9 CAP centres will handle calls from anywhere in France. All are 24/7.

### Germany
- Each Giftnotruf uses the regional area code + **19240** (e.g., 030 19240 for Berlin, 089 19240 for Munich). Erfurt is the exception: 0361 730730.

### Italy
- 116117 is rolling out region by region; not yet nationwide as of 2026-06. Confirmed active: Lombardy, Piedmont, Tuscany, Trento, parts of Sardinia and Veneto.

### Cyprus
- The appointed body is a regulatory/notification body only. For clinical poisoning emergencies, refer to the **Greek Poison Information Centre** (+30 210 779 3777) or call 112.

### Ukraine
- Active conflict affects operational continuity. The ambulance (103) or unified emergency line (112) is the safest fallback.

### Caucasus (AM, AZ, GE)
- These states are sometimes included in "Greater Europe" definitions. Included here for completeness but confidence is lower due to limited English-language sources.

### Microstates without own PIC (AD, LI, MC, SM, VA)
- All defer to their bordering country's service. AD → ES or FR; LI → AT; MC → FR; SM and VA → IT.

### Countries with NO confirmed dedicated poison line (low confidence)
Albania (AL), Bosnia & Herzegovina (BA), Belarus (BY), Kosovo (XK), Montenegro (ME), Moldova (MD — centre reportedly established but no public number), North Macedonia (MK — faculty-level centre, unclear 24/7 public access). For all these: call **112**, state "poisoning", and ask for toxicology.
