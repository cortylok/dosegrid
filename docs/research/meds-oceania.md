# Oceania OTC/PRN Medication Brand Names & Strengths
*Research for DoseGrid medication tracker — as-needed/short-course self-medication*
*Last updated: 2026-06-20 | Status: COMPLETE (8 markets)*

> **Note:** OTC status varies by country and changes over time. Items marked `(UNVERIFIED)` need confirmation.
> Users set their own dosing — this file lists brand names and available strengths/forms only.

## Australia (AU)
*Base dataset is Australian — this section confirms the most common brands. Australian medicine scheduling: S2 = Pharmacy Medicine, S3 = Pharmacist Only.*

### Analgesics / Antipyretics / NSAIDs
- **Paracetamol** → Panadol (500 mg tab/cap, Panadol Osteo 665 mg SR), Panamax (500 mg), Dymadon (500 mg, paediatric suspensions), Febridol (500 mg), Herron Paracetamol (500 mg), Chemists' Own / supermarket house brands (500 mg). Also liquids (e.g. 120 mg/5 mL, 240 mg/5 mL) and suppositories. OTC (pack-size restrictions apply). [TGA; Arthritis Australia; CHOICE]
- **Ibuprofen** → Nurofen (200 mg, 400 mg tab/cap, Nurofen Zavance/liquid caps), Advil (200 mg), Panafen (200 mg), Herron Blue, supermarket generics (200 mg). OTC up to 400 mg. [betterhealth.vic.gov.au; CHOICE]
- **Aspirin** → Aspro Clear (300 mg effervescent), Disprin (300 mg dispersible), Cartia / Astrix (100 mg low-dose EC for cardio). OTC. [Sydney Physio Clinic; arthritisaustralia.com.au]
- **Naproxen** → Naprogesic (275 mg tab — OTC for period pain), Naprosyn / Anaprox / Crysanal (higher strengths Rx). OTC 275 mg. [arthritisaustralia.com.au]
- **Diclofenac** → Voltaren (Voltaren Rapid 12.5 mg & 25 mg tabs OTC; Emulgel 1%/2.32% topical), Viclofen. Oral OTC at low strength; topical gel OTC. [betterhealth.vic.gov.au]
- **Mefenamic acid** → Ponstan (250 mg cap, 500 mg tab). S3 Pharmacist Only / Rx. [arthritisaustralia.com.au]

### Antihistamines
- **Fexofenadine** → Telfast (30 mg, 60 mg, 120 mg, 180 mg tab), Fexotabs, Xergic, supermarket generics. OTC. [Telfast AU; whichmedicine.com.au]
- **Cetirizine** → Zyrtec (10 mg tab, 1 mg/mL liquid, Zyrtec Kids), Zilarex, Alzene, generics. OTC. [whichmedicine.com.au]
- **Loratadine** → Claratyne (10 mg tab, syrup, Claratyne Kids), Lorano, Allereze, generics. OTC. [Claratyne AU; whichmedicine.com.au]
- **Promethazine** → Phenergan (10 mg, 25 mg tab, 5 mg/5 mL elixir). S2/S3. [healthdirect]
- **Chlorphenamine (chlorpheniramine)** → Demazin sold mainly as combination; Polaramine is dexchlorpheniramine (2 mg tab, 0.4 mg/mL syrup) — common AU form. OTC. [healthdirect] (UNVERIFIED standalone chlorphenamine brand)

### Antacids / GI / PPIs / H2
- **Antacids** → Mylanta (alum/mag hydroxide + simethicone, liquid/tab), Gaviscon (alginate, liquid/tab), Quick-Eze (calcium carbonate chewable), Rennie. OTC. [TGA-listed; pharmacy]
- **Omeprazole** → Losec (20 mg — Rx historically), Acimax, Probitor, Omepral; OTC 20 mg packs available. [healthdirect]
- **Esomeprazole** → Nexium (20 mg OTC pack, 40 mg Rx), Nexium 24HR. OTC 20 mg. [healthdirect]
- **Pantoprazole** → Somac (20 mg, 40 mg), Salpraz, Gastrostop is loperamide (do not confuse). OTC 20 mg / Rx 40 mg. [healthdirect]
- **Famotidine** → Pepcidine / Pepcid (20 mg, 40 mg), Pepzan. OTC 10–20 mg. [healthdirect]
- **Loperamide** → Imodium (2 mg cap), Gastro-Stop (2 mg), Diareze. OTC. [pharmacy]
- **Hyoscine butylbromide** → Buscopan (10 mg tab — antispasmodic). OTC (S2). [pharmacy]

### Decongestants / Cough / Cold
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Sudafed (60 mg tab), Codral Original Cold & Flu, Demazin (pseudoephedrine forms). S3 Pharmacist Only; ID/Project STOP recording required. [Pharmacy Guild; healthdirect]
- **Phenylephrine** → Sudafed PE, Codral PE, Demazin PE (combination cold & flu products). OTC (S2). [healthdirect]
- **Dextromethorphan** → Robitussin Dry Cough, Benadryl Dry (DM), Bisolvon Dry. OTC. [pharmacy]
- **Guaifenesin** → Robitussin Chesty, Mucinex (guaifenesin 600 mg/1200 mg ER), Benadryl Chesty. OTC. [pharmacy]
- **Bromhexine** → Bisolvon Chesty (8 mg tab, 4 mg/5 mL & 8 mg/5 mL liquid). OTC. [pharmacy]

### Sleep / Antiemetic / Motion
- **Doxylamine** → Restavit (25 mg tab), Dozile (25 mg), Restful Sleep. OTC (S3). Also in Mersyndol (paracetamol + codeine + doxylamine — Rx). [pharmacy]
- **Dimenhydrinate** → Dramamine (50 mg — limited AU availability), Andrews Travel-eze; more common AU motion-sickness brand is **Kwells** (hyoscine hydrobromide 0.3 mg) and **Travacalm** (hyoscine + antihistamines). OTC. [pharmacy]
- **Melatonin** → Circadin (2 mg prolonged-release — Rx/S4 in AU). Generally **Prescription** in Australia; OTC supplement melatonin not legally sold as a registered medicine. [TGA]
- **Metoclopramide** → Maxolon (10 mg tab). Prescription (S4). [healthdirect]
- **Prochlorperazine** → Stemetil (5 mg tab — Rx; some 5 mg S3). [pharmacy]

## New Zealand (NZ)
*NZ classifications: General Sale, Pharmacy-Only, Restricted (Pharmacist-Only), Prescription. Many NZ-specific brand names differ from Australia.*

### Analgesics / Antipyretics / NSAIDs
- **Paracetamol** → Panadol (500 mg tab), **Pamol** (NZ children's brand — 250 mg/5 mL suspension, orange; supplied by Aspen), Parapaed / Six Plus Parapaed (250 mg/5 mL), Paracare (withdrawn brand, replaced), Ethics Paracetamol, Noumed paracetamol. Liquids 120 mg/5 mL & 250 mg/5 mL; tabs 500 mg. General Sale / Pharmacy-Only. [Medsafe; Pamol NZ; akohiringa.co.nz]
- **Ibuprofen** → Nurofen (200 mg, 400 mg), Brufen (200/400 mg), Advil, I-Profen, **Fenpaed** (NZ children's ibuprofen suspension 100 mg/5 mL & 200 mg/5 mL). 200 mg General Sale/Pharmacy; 400 mg & 800 mg SR Prescription. [healthify.nz; Medsafe]
- **Aspirin** → Aspec, Cardiprin (100 mg low-dose EC), Disprin (300 mg dispersible). General Sale/Pharmacy. [Medsafe]
- **Naproxen** → Naprogesic (275 mg sodium — Pharmacy-Only for period pain), Noflam, Naprosyn (Rx higher strengths). [Medsafe class reports]
- **Diclofenac** → Voltaren (Voltaren Rapid 12.5 mg OTC; Emulgel topical 1%/2.32%), Apo-Diclo (Rx oral). Topical gel General Sale/Pharmacy. [Medsafe]
- **Mefenamic acid** → Ponstan (250 mg, 500 mg). Prescription. [Medsafe] (UNVERIFIED OTC status)

### Antihistamines
- **Cetirizine** → **Razene** (10 mg — common NZ brand), **Histaclear** (10 mg), Zista, Zetop, Zyrtec (10 mg, syrup). General Sale (5-pack) / Pharmacy-Only. [Medsafe; healthify.nz; Razene CMI]
- **Loratadine** → **Loraclear** (10 mg — common NZ brand), Claratyne (10 mg), Lora-tabs, Lorafix. General Sale/Pharmacy. [healthify.nz]
- **Fexofenadine** → Telfast (30/60/120/180 mg), **Fexaclear**, Fexofast. 30/60/120 mg General Sale (5-pack)/Pharmacy; 180 mg Pharmacy/Rx. [healthify.nz; Medsafe]
- **Levocetirizine** → Levrix (5 mg), Levoclear (5 mg). Pharmacy. [healthify.nz]
- **Desloratadine** → Aerius (5 mg). Pharmacy. [healthify.nz]
- **Promethazine** → Phenergan (10 mg, 25 mg tab, elixir). Pharmacy-Only after consultation. [healthify.nz; Medsafe]
- **Chlorphenamine** → mainly in combination cold products. (UNVERIFIED standalone NZ brand)

### Antacids / GI / PPIs / H2
- **Antacids** → Acidex (alginate), Gaviscon, Mylanta, Alu-Tab, Quick-Eze (calcium carbonate). General Sale/Pharmacy. [healthify.nz]
- **Omeprazole** → Losec (20 mg), Dr Reddy's Omeprazole (20 mg cap), Omezol. 20 mg available OTC/Pharmacist without prescription. [Medsafe datasheet; healthify.nz]
- **Pantoprazole** → Somac (20/40 mg), Dr Reddy's Pantoprazole. Pharmacy/Rx. [NZ Formulary] (UNVERIFIED OTC strength)
- **Loperamide** → Gastro-Stop (2 mg cap), Imodium (2 mg), Diocalm. Pharmacy/General Sale. [healthify.nz]
- **Hyoscine butylbromide** → Buscopan (10 mg tab). Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary]
- **Famotidine** → Pepcid / Pepcidtwo. Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary] (UNVERIFIED current NZ availability)

### Decongestants / Cough / Cold
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Sudafed and combination cold/flu products (with paracetamol or ibuprofen). **Returned to pharmacy sale in 2024** (Misuse of Drugs (Pseudoephedrine) Amendment Act 2024; reclassified Class C / Restricted Medicine — pharmacist sale, no Rx). 11 products provisionally consented April 2024. [Medsafe; legislation.govt.nz; Pharmacy Today]
- **Phenylephrine** → Sudafed PE, Codral PE, combination cold & flu products. General Sale/Pharmacy. [Medsafe]
- **Dextromethorphan** → Robitussin Dry, Benadryl Dry Forte. Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary]
- **Guaifenesin** → Robitussin Chesty, Mucinex. Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary]
- **Bromhexine** → Bisolvon Chesty (8 mg). Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary]

### Sleep / Antiemetic / Motion
- **Promethazine** → Phenergan (10/25 mg) used for travel sickness; available Pharmacy (≤10-tab packs for travel sickness). [Medsafe]
- **Dimenhydrinate** → Dramamine; available in NZ for travel sickness in packs ≤10 tablets. Pharmacy. [Medsafe class harmonisation]
- **Meclozine (meclizine)** → **Sea-Legs** (NZ travel-sickness brand, meclozine 12.5 mg). Pharmacy. [Medsafe class harmonisation]
- **Hyoscine hydrobromide** → Kwells. Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary] (UNVERIFIED brand currently in NZ)
- **Doxylamine** → Dozile (25 mg). Pharmacy. [NZ Formulary] (UNVERIFIED current NZ availability)
- **Melatonin** → Prescription medicine in NZ (e.g. Circadin 2 mg PR); not a General-Sale supplement. [Medsafe]
- **Metoclopramide** → Maxolon (10 mg). Prescription. [NZ Formulary]

## Papua New Guinea (PG)
*Import-driven market; no domestic manufacturing of these classes. Stock is mainly Australian, NZ, Indian and SE-Asian imports through Port Moresby/Lae pharmacies. Tighter regulatory environment than AU/NZ — even OTC items may be queried. Data is thin and brand availability fluctuates with import supply.*

### Analgesics / Antipyretics / NSAIDs
- **Paracetamol** → Panadol (500 mg tab — GSK import), plus generic paracetamol 500 mg from Indian suppliers. OTC at pharmacies. [papuanewguinea.travel; Global Rescue]
- **Ibuprofen** → Nurofen / generic ibuprofen 200 mg (imported). OTC but may be questioned at border. [Global Rescue]
- **Aspirin / Diclofenac / Mefenamic acid** → generic imports present in pharmacy supply chain; specific brands not consistently documented. (UNVERIFIED brand names)

### Other classes
- Antihistamines, antacids, cold/cough and antiemetic products are stocked as assorted Australian/NZ/Indian imports (e.g. cetirizine/loratadine generics, antacid suspensions). Specific brands not reliably documented in public sources. (UNVERIFIED)
- **Note:** PNG essential-medicines/supply-chain studies focus on antimalarials and antibiotics; OTC self-medication brand data is sparse. [PMC4020934]

## Fiji (FJ)
*Best-documented Pacific island market after AU/NZ. Pharmacies (Budget Pharmacy, Medisure, BSP HealthCare network) stock Australian, NZ and Indian imports. Codeine and tramadol are controlled. A government Free Medicine Program supplies generics through public pharmacies.*

### Analgesics / Antipyretics / NSAIDs
- **Paracetamol** → Panadol (500 mg tab), Panamax (500 mg tab — cheaper generic). OTC. [tripadvisor Fiji forum pricing; Budget Pharmacy]
- **Ibuprofen** → Advil (200 mg), Brufen (400 mg). OTC. [tripadvisor Fiji forum]
- **Aspirin / Diclofenac / Mefenamic acid / Naproxen** → present on the Fiji Essential Medicines List (generic paracetamol, ibuprofen, aspirin, diclofenac, mefenamic acid listed); retail brands largely Indian/AU generics. [Fiji Essential Medicines List 4th Ed. 2015]

### Other classes
- Antihistamines (cetirizine, loratadine, chlorphenamine, promethazine), antacids, omeprazole, loperamide and cold/cough products appear on the Essential Medicines List as generics and are stocked at retail pharmacies as assorted imports. Specific retail brand names beyond the above not reliably documented. [Fiji EML 2015; BSP HealthCare Approved Medication List 2020] (UNVERIFIED retail brands)

## Samoa (WS)
*Small import-driven market; OTC drugs restricted to pharmacies. Generally follows NZ guidance; paracetamol consumer factsheets exist in Samoan. Supply is predominantly NZ/Australian imports plus WHO Essential Medicines List generics.*

- **Paracetamol** → Panadol and generic paracetamol 500 mg (NZ/AU imports). OTC at pharmacies. [Starship Pacific medicines; WHO/Samoa EML 2022]
- **Ibuprofen, aspirin, antihistamines (cetirizine/loratadine), antacids, loperamide** → present as generics on Samoa Essential Medicines List 2022; retail brands are assorted NZ/AU/Indian imports, not individually documented. [Samoa Essential Medicines List 2022 (WHO)] (UNVERIFIED retail brands)

## Solomon Islands (SB)
*Very thin, import-driven market. OTC medicines available only in pharmacies (limited number, mainly Honiara). Supply mainly Australian/NZ and WHO EML generics.*

- **Paracetamol** → generic paracetamol 500 mg / Panadol (imported). OTC in pharmacies. [Starship Pacific medicines availability; Global Rescue]
- Other OTC classes (ibuprofen, antihistamines, antacids, loperamide) present as imported generics; specific brand names not documented in public sources. (UNVERIFIED) [Starship Pacific medicines availability]

## Vanuatu (VU)
*Small import-driven market drawing on both Australian/NZ (English-speaking) and French (francophone) supply chains. Pharmacy-restricted OTC sale.*

- **Paracetamol** → Panadol / Doliprane (French-brand paracetamol may appear via francophone supply) / generic 500 mg. OTC in pharmacies. (Doliprane UNVERIFIED for VU specifically) [Global Rescue; trade import data]
- Other OTC classes (ibuprofen, antihistamines, antacids) are assorted Australian/NZ/French imports; specific brand names not reliably documented. (UNVERIFIED)

## Tonga (TO)
*Small import-driven market; generally follows NZ guidance, OTC drugs restricted to pharmacies. Paracetamol consumer factsheets exist in Tongan. Supply mainly NZ/Australian imports.*

- **Paracetamol** → Panadol and generic paracetamol 500 mg (NZ/AU imports). OTC in pharmacies. [Starship Pacific medicines; Global Rescue]
- Other OTC classes (ibuprofen, antihistamines, antacids, loperamide) present as imported generics; specific retail brand names not documented in public sources. (UNVERIFIED)
