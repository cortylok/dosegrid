> **Purpose:** Reference for the DoseGrid PRN medication tracker.
> **Scope:** Over-the-counter / self-managed / short-course medicines only.

## United Kingdom (GB)

**Regulatory note:** Medicines are classed as GSL (General Sales List — supermarkets/shops), P (Pharmacy-only), or POM (Prescription Only). Most OTC analgesics are P-class. Diclofenac tablets were removed from OTC sale (cardiovascular risk); topical diclofenac gel (Voltarol) remains OTC.

### Analgesics & Anti-inflammatories
- **Paracetamol** → Panadol (500 mg tab, 500 mg effervescent, 120 mg/5 mL paediatric susp), Calpol (120 mg/5 mL & 250 mg/5 mL susp, paediatric), Hedex (500 mg tab). Generic 500 mg tabs widely available GSL. OTC. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/paracetamol-for-adults/)
- **Ibuprofen** → Nurofen (200 mg, 400 mg tabs; 100 mg/5 mL susp paediatric), Brufen (200 mg, 400 mg), Calprofen (paediatric susp 100 mg/5 mL). Generic 200/400 mg tabs GSL. OTC. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/ibuprofen-for-adults/)
- **Aspirin** → Anadin (300 mg tab, 75 mg enteric-coated), Aspirin (various generics 300 mg, 75 mg), Disprin (300 mg effervescent). OTC. [Ashcroft Pharmacy](https://www.ashcroftpharmacy.co.uk/blog/understanding-otc-nsaids-best-options-uses-and-more/)
- **Naproxen** → Feminax Ultra (250 mg gastro-resistant tab, licensed for menstrual pain), Naprosyn Pain Relief 250 mg gastro-resistant tab (Maxwellia — first dedicated OTC naproxen). P-class pharmacy only. OTC. [Pharmacy Business](https://www.pharmacy.biz/uk-first-otc-naproxen-maxwellia/)
- **Diclofenac (oral)** → PRESCRIPTION ONLY as of 2015 (cardiovascular risk reclassification). [GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/diclofenac-tablets-now-only-available-as-a-prescription-medicine)
- **Diclofenac (topical gel)** → Voltarol Gel (1% diclofenac, 2.32% diclofenac); Voltarol Pain-eze Emulgel; Diclac Gel. OTC P-class. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/diclofenac/)

### Antihistamines
- **Cetirizine** → Piriteze (10 mg tab), Zirtek (10 mg tab, 1 mg/mL oral soln). Generic 10 mg widely OTC. [Bolt Pharmacy](https://www.boltpharmacy.co.uk/guide/allergy-medication-similar-to-fexofenadine)
- **Loratadine** → Clarityn (10 mg tab, 5 mg/5 mL syrup). Generic 10 mg OTC. [Chemist 4U](https://www.chemist-4-u.com/guides/hayfever/best-antihistamines/)
- **Fexofenadine** → Telfast 120 mg (OTC — for allergic rhinitis), Allevia 120 mg, Treathay 120 mg. NOTE: 180 mg strength (urticaria) is POM. [Dr Fox](https://www.doctorfox.co.uk/hay-fever/antihistamine-tablets.html)
- **Chlorphenamine (Chlorpheniramine)** → Piriton (4 mg tab, 2 mg/5 mL syrup). Classic sedating antihistamine. OTC. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/chlorphenamine-including-piriton/)
- **Promethazine** → Phenergan (10 mg, 25 mg tabs; 5 mg/5 mL elixir). OTC P-class; also used for travel sickness and short-term sleep. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/promethazine/)
- **Diphenhydramine** → Nytol (25 mg tab), Benadryl Allergy Relief (acrivastine — different molecule, but same class). OTC sleep use. [Manual.co](https://www.manual.co/blog/what-over-the-counter-sleeping-pills-can-you-get-in-the-uk)
- **Acrivastine** → Benadryl Allergy Relief (8 mg capsule). Fast-acting non-sedating. OTC.
- **Desloratadine** → Neoclarityn — PRESCRIPTION ONLY in UK (POM). [UNVERIFIED]

### Antacids & GI Acid Medicines
- **Calcium carbonate / alginate antacids** → Rennie (calcium carbonate + magnesium carbonate, chewable tab), Gaviscon Original (sodium alginate 500 mg/10 mL liquid; tablet), Gaviscon Advance (sodium alginate 1000 mg/10 mL — stronger), Tums (calcium carbonate 500/750 mg, US import widely available). OTC GSL. [Which?](https://www.which.co.uk/news/article/gaviscon-vs-rennie-and-other-heartburn-remedies-a36tY5f0st75)
- **Famotidine** → PRESCRIPTION ONLY in UK. (Available via online pharmacy consultation.) [Cloud Pharmacy](https://www.cloudpharmacy.co.uk/online-doctor/acid-reflux-treatments/famotidine/)
- **Omeprazole** → Losec (10 mg, 20 mg caps — OTC), Pyrocalm (20 mg). Generic omeprazole 20 mg OTC (short-term ≤4 weeks). OTC P-class. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/omeprazole/)
- **Esomeprazole** → Nexium Control (20 mg caps). OTC P-class. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/esomeprazole/)
- **Pantoprazole** → Generally POM in UK; limited OTC availability. [UNVERIFIED]

### Cold, Cough & Decongestants
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Sudafed Decongestant (60 mg tab). P-class pharmacy only, ID required, quantity limits. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/pseudoephedrine/)
- **Pseudoephedrine combinations** → Benylin Day & Night (pseudoephedrine + paracetamol), Actifed (triprolidine + pseudoephedrine), Non-Drowsy Sinutab. P-class.
- **Phenylephrine** → Sudafed Non-Drowsy Decongestant (10 mg). GSL. Various combination cold products.
- **Dextromethorphan (DXM)** → Benylin Dry Coughs (7.5 mg/5 mL), Robitussin Dry Cough (15 mg/5 mL). P-class. [Medicines.org.uk](https://www.medicines.org.uk/emc/)
- **Guaifenesin** → Benylin Chesty Coughs, Robitussin Chesty Cough. OTC.
- **Bromhexine** → Bisolvon (8 mg tab, syrup). P-class. (UNVERIFIED — less commonly sold; Bisolvon is a Boehringer brand primarily available in other EU markets.)

### Travel Sickness / Motion Sickness
- **Cinnarizine** → Stugeron (15 mg tab). P-class. [PharmSee](https://pharmsee.co.uk/blog/post/travel-sickness-medicines-compared-pharmacy-uk-2026)
- **Hyoscine hydrobromide (Scopolamine)** → Kwells (0.3 mg tab), Joy-Rides (0.15 mg tab), Scopoderm TTS (1.5 mg transdermal patch — P-class). [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/hyoscine-hydrobromide/)
- **Promethazine** → Avomine (25 mg tab). P-class. Travel sickness & sedating antihistamine.
- **Dimenhydrinate** → Not commonly sold as branded OTC in UK; Dramamine not widely distributed. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Meclizine** → Not available OTC in UK; Sea-Legs contains meclizine (UNVERIFIED — may have been discontinued). [UNVERIFIED]

### Antidiarrhoeal
- **Loperamide** → Imodium (2 mg capsule/tablet/liqui-gel/melts), generic loperamide 2 mg widely OTC. [My Pharmacy](https://www.mypharmacy.co.uk/diarrhoea/loperamide-anti-diarrhoea-capsules/)

### Migraine
- **Sumatriptan** → Imigran Recovery (50 mg tab — OTC if previously GP-diagnosed migraine). P-class. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/sumatriptan/)
- **Aspirin + Metoclopramide** → Migraleve (paracetamol + codeine + buclizine), MigraMax (aspirin + metoclopramide — POM). [UNVERIFIED for OTC status of MigraMax]

### Sleep Aids
- **Diphenhydramine** → Nytol (25 mg, 50 mg — One-A-Night), Sominex (20 mg). OTC. [Manual.co](https://www.manual.co/blog/what-over-the-counter-sleeping-pills-can-you-get-in-the-uk)
- **Promethazine** → Phenergan (25 mg) — licensed for short-term sleep. P-class.
- **Doxylamine** → Not widely available OTC in UK as standalone sleep aid. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Melatonin** → Prescription only in UK (Circadin 2 mg — licensed 55+ adults; Slenyto 1 mg/5 mg — paediatric). All melatonin is classified as a medicine (POM) in UK since 1995 — unauthorised food supplement products also sold online but not legally authorised. [NHS](https://www.nhs.uk/medicines/melatonin/) [ScienceInsights](https://scienceinsights.org/is-melatonin-legal-in-europe-country-by-country-rules/)

## Ireland (IE)

**Regulatory note:** Irish Medicines Board / HPRA regulates. Similar system to UK: GSL, P, POM. Pharmacy chains include Boots, LloydsPharmacy, McCabes.

### Analgesics & Anti-inflammatories

- **Ibuprofen** → Nurofen (200 mg, 400 mg), Nurofen Plus (200 mg ibuprofen + codeine — P-class), Brufen (200/400/600 mg — higher strengths POM), Buplex (200/400 mg), Easofen (200/400 mg), Phorpain (200/400 mg), Melfen (200/400 mg), Brupro (200 mg). OTC. [Inish Pharmacy](https://www.inishpharmacy.com/collections/pain-relief)
- **Aspirin** → Generic 300 mg, 75 mg enteric-coated. OTC P-class.
- **Diclofenac (topical)** → Diclac Gel, Voltarol Gel. OTC. [Voltarol IE](https://www.voltarol.ie/)
- **Naproxen** → Feminax Ultra (250 mg) — P-class. (UNVERIFIED availability vs UK version)
- **Codeine combinations** → Solpadeine (paracetamol 500 mg + caffeine 30 mg + codeine 8 mg), Solpadeine Max (paracetamol 500 mg + codeine 12.8 mg), Nurofen Plus. Most popular OTC sellers in Ireland. P-class. [Medical Independent](https://www.medicalindependent.ie/clinical-news/pain-management-over-the-counter-otc-remedies/)

### Antihistamines
- **Cetirizine** → Piriteze, Zirtek (10 mg). OTC.
- **Loratadine** → Clarityn (10 mg). OTC.
- **Fexofenadine** → Telfast 120 mg. OTC P-class.

### Antacids & GI
- **Antacids** → Rennie, Gaviscon, Gaviscon Advance. OTC.
- **Omeprazole** → Generic 20 mg OTC P-class.
- **Loperamide** → Imodium (2 mg). OTC.

### Cold & Flu
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Sudafed (60 mg). P-class (pharmacy ID restrictions).
- **Phenylephrine** → Sudafed Non-Drowsy, Lemsip Max (combination). GSL.

### Travel Sickness
- **Cinnarizine** → Stugeron (15 mg). OTC P-class.
- **Hyoscine** → Kwells (0.3 mg). OTC P-class.
- **Promethazine** → Avomine (25 mg). OTC P-class.

### Sleep & Migraine
- **Diphenhydramine** → Nytol (25 mg). OTC.
- **Sumatriptan** → Imigran Recovery (50 mg) — P-class, previously diagnosed migraine only.
- **Melatonin** → POM in Ireland. [UNVERIFIED]

## Germany (DE)

**Regulatory note:** Apothekenpflichtig = pharmacy-only (no Rx needed). Rezeptpflichtig = prescription required. Freiverkäuflich = freely sold (supermarkets, kiosks). Most active OTC medicines are apothekenpflichtig. PZN = German pharmaceutical number. Online pharmacies: DocMorris, Shop-Apotheke, medpex.

### Analgesics & Anti-inflammatories
- **Paracetamol** → Ben-u-ron (500 mg tab, 125/250/500/1000 mg supp, 200 mg/5 mL susp — child & adult), Paracetamol ratiopharm (500 mg, 1000 mg), Paracetamol AL (500 mg), Paracetamol Hexal (500 mg). OTC up to 10 g/pack. [GermanyHandbook](https://germanyhandbook.com/common-medications-and-their-german-brand-names-a-quick-reference/)
- **Ibuprofen** → Nurofen (200/400 mg tab, paediatric susp), IBU-ratiopharm (200/400/600 mg — higher doses vary), Dolormin (400 mg), IBU-LYSIN-ratiopharm 400 mg (faster-acting lysine salt), Aktren (400 mg), Ibuflam (400/600 mg). OTC up to 400 mg single dose; 600 mg may require Rx in some formulations. [Gesundheitsinformation.de](https://www.gesundheitsinformation.de/rezeptfreie-schmerzmittel-sicher-anwenden.html)
- **Aspirin / ASS** → Aspirin (300 mg, 500 mg — Bayer brand), ASS ratiopharm (100/300/500 mg), Aspirin Complex (500 mg ASS + 30 mg pseudoephedrine — combination cold). OTC. [Römer Apotheke](https://www.roemer-apotheken-mainz.de/magazin/Schmerzmittel-im-Vergleich-was-ASS-IBU-oder-Paracetamol-unterscheidet-5103)
- **Naproxen** → Dolormin für Frauen (250 mg gastro-resistant, menstrual pain). OTC. Generic naproxen 250/500 mg also apothekenpflichtig without Rx.
- **Diclofenac (oral)** → Prescription required for oral diclofenac in Germany. [UNVERIFIED current status — was available OTC in past]
- **Diclofenac (topical)** → Voltaren Schmerzgel (1%, 2% — various pack sizes), Voltaren Emulgel, Diclofenac ratiopharm gel (1%). OTC. [Germany Handbook](https://germanyhandbook.com/)
- **Dexibuprofen** → OTC up to 200 mg single dose / 600 mg/day (recent OTC switch). Apothekenpflichtig.
- **Thomapyrin** → Paracetamol 250 mg + Aspirin 250 mg + Caffeine 50 mg tab. Classic combination analgesic. OTC. [AOK Magazin](https://www.aok.de/pk/magazin/koerper-psyche/gehirn-nerven/rezeptfreie-schmerzmittel-der-grosse-check/)

### Antihistamines
- **Cetirizine** → Cetirizin Hexal (10 mg tab), Cetirizin ratiopharm (10 mg), Cetirizin-ADGC (10 mg), Zyrtec (10 mg). OTC apothekenpflichtig. [GermanyHandbook](https://germanyhandbook.com/)
- **Loratadine** → Loratadin AL (10 mg tab), Loratadin ratiopharm (10 mg), Lisino (10 mg). OTC apothekenpflichtig. [AllAboutBerlin](https://allaboutberlin.com/guides/hay-fever)
- **Fexofenadine** → Telfast (120 mg, 180 mg), Fexofenadin ratiopharm. OTC apothekenpflichtig. [GermanyHandbook](https://germanyhandbook.com/)
- **Desloratadine** → Aerius, Desloratadin ratiopharm (5 mg). Prescription required in Germany. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Levocetirizine** → ADGC Levocetirizin (5 mg), Levocetirizin ratiopharm (5 mg). OTC apothekenpflichtig.
- **Dimetinden (Dimethindene)** → Fenistil (1 mg/mL drops oral; gel topical — for insect bites, skin reactions). OTC. [ADGC](https://www.adgc.de/)
- **Chlorphenamine** → Less commonly branded in Germany; generally superseded by 2nd-gen AH. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Promethazine** → Atosil, Proneurin — generally Rx in Germany for systemic use. [UNVERIFIED]

### Antacids & GI Acid Medicines
- **Calcium carbonate / Mg antacids** → Rennie (680 mg calcium carbonate + 80 mg magnesium carbonate, chewable), Talcid (hydrotalcite 500 mg chewable), Maalox (aluminium hydroxide + magnesium hydroxide). OTC. [medikamente-per-klick](https://www.medikamente-per-klick.de/apotheke/medikamentenglossar/antazida/)
- **Famotidine** → Famotidin (40 mg) — apothekenpflichtig without Rx since 2019. OTC apothekenpflichtig. [Klinik-gastroenterologie.de](https://www.klinik-gastroenterologie.de/)
- **Omeprazole** → Omeprazol ratiopharm 10 mg OTC (since 2009), Omeprazol 20 mg OTC in pack ≤14 tabs (since 2018), various generics. OTC apothekenpflichtig. [ZAVA](https://www.zavamed.com/de/medikamente-gegen-sodbrennen.html)
- **Pantoprazole** → Pantoprazol ratiopharm 20 mg (OTC since 2009, pack ≤14 tabs). Pantoprazol 40 mg is Rx. OTC apothekenpflichtig. [Apotheke ADHOC](https://www.apotheke-adhoc.de/)
- **Simethicone (gas)** → Sab simplex (suspension), Espumisan (capsule). OTC.

### Cold, Cough & Decongestants
- **Pseudoephedrine** → Aspirin Complex (with ASS), Wick Formel 44 combinations. Quantity-controlled, apothekenpflichtig. Note: products containing pseudoephedrine restricted since 2009 Combat Methamphetamine Epidemic Act equivalents in EU.
- **Phenylephrine** → Wick DayMed (with paracetamol), NasenDuo nasal spray. OTC.
- **Xylometazoline (nasal)** → Olynth (0.05%, 0.1% nasal spray), Nasengel. OTC. (Decongestant nasal spray — not oral)
- **Oxymetazoline (nasal)** → Nasivin (0.05% nasal spray). OTC.
- **Dextromethorphan** → Part of combination cold products (Wick DayMed Nacht, various). Apothekenpflichtig.
- **Guaifenesin** → Included in some combination cough products. [UNVERIFIED standalone brand]
- **Bromhexine** → Available in Germany (8 mg tab). Generic and branded (Bisolvon). OTC apothekenpflichtig.
- **Ambroxol** → Mucosolvan (30 mg tab, syrup), Ambrobene. OTC. (Mucolytic — commonly used in Germany)
- **Ivy leaf extract** → Prospan (cough syrup — herbal). OTC.
- **Sinupret** → Gentian/primrose/elderflower/verbena/sorrel combination (sinus/cold herbal). OTC. Very commonly used in Germany.

### Travel Sickness
- **Dimenhydrinate** → Vomex A (50 mg tab, 50 mg/5 mL susp, 40 mg/5 mL susp, 150 mg suppositories — various strengths), Vomacur (dimenhydrinate 50 mg). OTC apothekenpflichtig. [Vomex](https://vomex.de/)
- **Diphenhydramine** → Sedaplus (25 mg, for sleep); standalone less common for travel sickness vs dimenhydrinate.
- **Cinnarizine** → Less commonly marketed in Germany; not main-line OTC. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Scopolamine (Hyoscine)** → Scopoderm TTS patch (Rx in Germany). [UNVERIFIED OTC status]

### Antidiarrhoeal
- **Loperamide** → Imodium akut (2 mg caps/melts), Loperamid ratiopharm, generic loperamide 2 mg. OTC apothekenpflichtig. [Shop-Apotheke](https://www.shop-apotheke.com/)
- **Oral rehydration** → Elotrans (ORS sachets). OTC.

### Migraine
- **Sumatriptan** → Prescription only (Rx) in Germany. (OTC switch was blocked by Bundesrat in 2013; renewed attempt as of 2024 — not yet approved.) [Apotheke ADHOC](https://www.apotheke-adhoc.de/nachrichten/detail/pharmazie/sumatriptan-neuer-anlauf-fuer-otc-switch-verschreibungspflicht/)

### Sleep Aids
- **Diphenhydramine** → Sedaplus (25 mg tab), Halbmond (25 mg), Betadorm (25 mg). OTC apothekenpflichtig. [DocMorris](https://www.docmorris.de/)
- **Doxylamine** → Hoggar Night (12.5/25 mg doxylamine succinate). OTC apothekenpflichtig. [Shop-Apotheke](https://www.shop-apotheke.com/)
- **Melatonin** → Various OTC melatonin 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 1.9 mg products (food supplement status ≤1 mg; medicinal ≥1 mg may be apothekenpflichtig). Melatonin products widely available at pharmacies and Drogerie (DM, Rossmann). [DocMorris](https://www.docmorris.de/)
- **Valerian** → Baldriparan, Laif (combination). OTC. Herbal sleep aid very popular in Germany.

## France (FR)

**Regulatory note:** Since Jan 2020, paracetamol, ibuprofen, and aspirin must be kept behind the pharmacy counter (no longer libre accès on shelves) — still no Rx required, but must ask the pharmacist. Since Dec 2024, pseudoephedrine-containing cold medicines require a prescription. OTC in France means sans ordonnance but dispensed at counter.

### Analgesics & Anti-inflammatories
- **Paracetamol** → Doliprane (1000 mg tab, 500 mg tab, 100/150/300 mg supp, 1000 mg sachets, 200 mg/mL oral soln), Dafalgan (500 mg, 1000 mg tab; effervescent sachets; 10 mg/mL soln), Efferalgan (500 mg, 1000 mg effervescent tab; codéiné variant = Rx), Panadol (500 mg — less dominant). OTC sans ordonnance. [VIDAL](https://www.vidal.fr/actualites/24050/)
- **Ibuprofen** → Advil (200 mg, 400 mg tab; children's syrup), Nurofen (200/400 mg tab; paediatric susp), Spedifen (400 mg granulés effervescents — fast-acting). OTC sans ordonnance. [Pharmacie des Drakkars](https://www.pharmaciedesdrakkars.com/)
- **Aspirin** → Aspégic (100/250/500/1000 mg sachets — dispersible), Aspirine pH 8 (Upsa, 500 mg tab), Aspro (500 mg), Aspirine 500 mg Bayer. OTC sans ordonnance.
- **Naproxen** → Naproxène — generally Rx in France for most indications. Aleve (naproxen 220 mg) available in some pharmacies but status uncertain. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Diclofenac (oral)** → Rx required in France.
- **Diclofenac (topical)** → Voltaren Emulgel (1% diclofenac). OTC. [gogofrance.com](https://gogofrance.com/)
- **Ketoprofen (topical)** → Fastum Gel (2.5% ketoprofen). OTC sans ordonnance. (Common in France)
- **Algodol Caféine** → Paracetamol 500 mg + caffeine 50 mg. OTC combination analgesic.
- **Spasfon** → Phloroglucinol 80 mg — antispasmodic (abdominal cramps, dysmenorrhoea). OTC.

### Antihistamines
- **Cetirizine** → Zyrtec (10 mg tab, 1 mg/mL oral soln), Alairgix (10 mg). OTC sans ordonnance. [gogofrance.com](https://gogofrance.com/)
- **Loratadine** → Clarityne (10 mg tab), Loratadine générique. OTC.
- **Fexofenadine** → Telfast (120/180 mg). OTC for 120 mg; 180 mg status varies. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Lévocétirizine** → Xyzall (5 mg tab). OTC sans ordonnance.
- **Desloratadine** → Aerius (5 mg tab). Rx in France. [UNVERIFIED — was Rx; status may have changed]
- **Ebastine** → Kestin (10 mg). OTC [UNVERIFIED]
- **Promethazine** → Phénergan (5 mg/5 mL elixir, 25 mg tab). P/Rx depending on indication. [UNVERIFIED]
- **Diphénhydramine (topical)** → Butix gel. OTC (insect bites, urticaria).

### Antacids & GI
- **Antacids** → Rennie (calcium carbonate + magnesium carbonate, chewable), Maalox (aluminium hydroxide + magnesium hydroxide, chewable/gel sachets), Gaviscon (sodium alginate, liquid/tab). OTC. [gogofrance.com](https://gogofrance.com/)

- **Esomeprazole** → Inexium Control (20 mg) — OTC sans ordonnance (short-term heartburn). [UNVERIFIED]

- **Simethicone** → Imonogas (simethicone 80 mg cap, chewable). OTC.

## Key Cross-Market Notes

1. **Diclofenac oral:** Oral diclofenac is OTC *topical only* in UK (tablets moved to Rx 2015). In Italy it is OTC at 25 mg (Voltadvance). In Germany and Netherlands also restricted/Rx. Always verify locally.
2. **Pseudoephedrine:** France banned pseudoephedrine from OTC (Dec 2024 — Actifed, Dolirhume, Rhinadvil, Humex Rhume all now Rx). UK, Germany, Netherlands, Poland have pharmacy-only restrictions with quantity/ID limits. Spain limits it to combination products (Clarityne Plus, Reactine). Scandinavia: largely unavailable OTC.
3. **Sumatriptan:** OTC in UK (Imigran Recovery 50 mg — previously-diagnosed migraine only, P-class) and Netherlands (UA). Prescription required in Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Ireland, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Austria, Switzerland, Poland, CZ, Romania, Greece, Portugal, Belgium.
4. **Melatonin:** Prescription medicine (Rx/POM) in UK (POM since 1995), Ireland, Norway, Sweden, Denmark. OTC as food supplement in: Germany (≤1.9 mg widely sold), France (≤2 mg supplement), Spain (OTC supplement + Dormidina Melatonin brand), Italy (Melatonina supplement OTC), Poland (supplement OTC), Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, Switzerland, Portugal, Greece, CZ, Romania (various supplement brands OTC). Scandinavian countries (SE/NO/DK) classify melatonin as a medicine not a food supplement — Finland status unconfirmed but likely Rx by similarity. [ScienceInsights](https://scienceinsights.org/is-melatonin-legal-in-europe-country-by-country-rules/)
5. **Famotidine:** OTC in Germany (Famotidin 40 mg since 2019), Austria, Spain (Pepcid), USA — but **Prescription Only in UK**. Verify country-by-country.
6. **Desloratadine / Levocetirizine:** Generally Rx across EU markets, unlike cetirizine/loratadine which are widely OTC. Exception: desloratadine may be OTC in Spain.
7. **Doxylamine as OTC sleep aid:** Widely OTC in France (Donormyl 15 mg — UPSA brand; limited to 6 boxes/month), Spain (Dormidina 12.5/25 mg), Germany (Hoggar Night 25 mg). Less available in UK (market dominated by diphenhydramine — Nytol). Not available OTC in Scandinavia. Prescription required in some markets.
8. **Bromhexine / Ambroxol / Acetylcysteine:** Very commonly available OTC across Central and Southern Europe (Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Italy, Spain, Poland, CZ, Romania, Greece) — less common in UK/Ireland where these mucolytics are less culturally entrenched; UK relies more on guaifenesin (Robitussin, Benylin Chesty Cough).
9. **Cinnarizine (Stugeron):** OTC travel sickness medicine widely available in UK/Ireland (15 mg) and throughout most of Europe (25 mg in many EU markets). More familiar to European consumers than US-dominant Dramamine (dimenhydrinate). Germany and France prefer dimenhydrinate (Vomex A, Nausicalm).
10. **Metamizole (Dipyrone/Algocalmin):** OTC or quasi-OTC in Romania, Poland (available), Germany (rarely). Banned/withdrawn in UK, USA, Sweden, Japan due to agranulocytosis risk. DoseGrid should flag this as geographically limited with safety warning.
11. **Bilastine:** A newer non-sedating antihistamine from Spanish pharma Faes Farma; OTC in Spain (Ibis, Astrilax, Bilastine Tevagen — 20 mg). Available in other EU markets but status varies.
12. **Naproxen OTC variances:** UK: Feminax Ultra 250 mg (menstrual), Naprosyn Pain Relief 250 mg. Norway: Napren 250 mg. Sweden: Pronaxen 250 mg. Italy: Momendol 220 mg. Spain: generally Rx. Most other EU markets: 220–250 mg OTC at apotek/farmácia/farmacie.
13. **Codeine combinations:** OTC codeine combinations (Solpadeine, Nurofen Plus) widely sold in Ireland (P-class). In UK also available P-class but pharmacist counselling required. In most EU countries codeine is Rx even in combination.
14. **Buscopan (hyoscine butylbromide):** OTC antispasmodic for GI cramps; Boehringer Ingelheim brand widely available OTC across EU (Italy, Germany, Spain, France, Belgium, Poland, Austria, Switzerland, Netherlands, etc.) at 10 mg tabs. Buscopan Compositum (with paracetamol) available in some markets. UK: OTC. [Drugs.com international](https://www.drugs.com/international/buscopan.html)
15. **Omeprazole / PPIs OTC:** Short-term OTC use (≤4 weeks heartburn/GERD symptoms) is widely available at 20 mg in most EU markets. 40 mg is generally Rx. Pantoprazole 20 mg is OTC in Germany, Austria, and some others. Esomeprazole (Nexium Control 20 mg) is OTC across most EU markets.
16. **Dimetindene (Fenistil):** A first-generation antihistamine in drop form (1 mg/mL oral) and gel (0.1% topical). OTC widely across EU (Germany, Switzerland, Austria, Italy, Spain, Greece, CZ, Romania, Poland). Not commonly available in UK/IE/Nordic.
17. **Ambroxol vs bromhexine vs acetylcysteine (mucolytics):** All three are widely OTC across Central/Southern/Eastern Europe. Mucosolvan (ambroxol) is the most ubiquitous EU brand. Bisolvon (bromhexine) is Boehringer brand. ACC/Fluimucil (acetylcysteine) very popular in Italy, Portugal, Greece, CZ, Romania. UK uses guaifenesin (Benylin Chesty Cough, Robitussin) instead.
18. **Ketoprofen:** Oral OTC in Italy (Oki/Fastum 25–40 mg), Netherlands (25 mg). Topical OTC widely in France (Fastum Gel 2.5%), Spain (Fastum Gel), Italy. Rx or not available at any dose in UK, Ireland, Scandinavia.
